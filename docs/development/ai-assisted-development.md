# AI-Assisted Development Policy

AI-assisted coding may be used in Delphi Commons development, but human maintainers are responsible for every committed change.

## Human Responsibility

Maintainers must review, test, and accept AI-assisted code before it is committed. AI output is never authoritative and does not replace engineering judgment, ethical review, security review, accessibility review, or methods review.

## Governance Constraints

AI-assisted development must not weaken:

- the Ethical Governance Charter;
- the AI Governance & Human-in-the-Loop Thin Spec;
- consent and withdrawal protections;
- participant privacy;
- identity/response separation;
- auditability;
- neutral feedback;
- non-consensus reporting;
- human approval of participant-facing AI-assisted content.

## Data Restrictions

Do not use real participant data in AI coding prompts. Do not paste:

- participant names;
- emails or phone numbers;
- consent records;
- identity mappings;
- raw response data;
- sensitive study exports;
- audit logs containing sensitive details;
- API keys, tokens, or credentials.

Use synthetic examples or minimized, non-identifying snippets.

## Required Review

AI-assisted changes should be checked for:

- correctness;
- security;
- privacy;
- accessibility;
- test coverage;
- licensing compatibility;
- consistency with Delphi-method fidelity;
- consistency with human-in-the-loop AI governance.

## No Automated Authority

AI may assist with drafts, refactors, tests, documentation, and review prompts. It must not decide consensus rules, participant-facing wording, study outcomes, or governance exceptions without human review.
