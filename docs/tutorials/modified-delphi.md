# Modified Delphi Tutorial

This tutorial walks through a modified Delphi flow using synthetic data only. It is not approval for real human-subjects launch.

## Purpose

A modified Delphi study begins with a predefined item set instead of using a fully open-ended Round 1. This may be appropriate when items come from literature, policy documents, prior studies, or expert drafting.

## 1. Document Replacement Of Round 1

If Round 1 is replaced or constrained, document the reason. The study record should explain:

- source of the initial item set;
- why an open-ended Round 1 is not used;
- what bias this may introduce;
- how participants can still provide comments, dissent, or proposed additions where protocol allows.

## 2. Preserve Item Provenance

Each item should have a source label:

- literature-derived;
- researcher-added;
- panel-generated;
- AI-assisted draft, human approved.

Do not imply that an AI-assisted item has independent authority. Participant-facing labels should make clear that AI-assisted drafts were human approved.

## 3. Bias Warning

Modified Delphi designs can shape the outcome by defining the item set before participants respond. Reports should disclose that the initial item set may influence what can reach consensus.

## 4. Configure Consent And Consensus Rule

As with classical Delphi, configure participant information, consent, and the consensus rule before launch. The consensus rule should be predefined and locked.

## 5. Ratings

Participants rate or rank the predefined items. The interface should preserve independent judgment and avoid language that encourages matching the group.

## 6. Feedback

Later rounds may show neutral controlled feedback:

- group median;
- response spread or IQR;
- distribution;
- neutral summary where approved;
- participant prior response where configured.

Participants may retain or revise their view.

## 7. Reporting Limitations

Final reports should disclose:

- the study used a modified Delphi design;
- the item set source;
- item provenance;
- bias and scope limits;
- non-consensus results;
- attrition and response rates;
- that consensus does not establish correctness.

## 8. Exports

Exports should include provenance, consensus rule, response rates, non-consensus outcomes, and limitations. Do not export participant identities unless a separate approved permission model allows it.
