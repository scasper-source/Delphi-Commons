# Classical Delphi Tutorial

This tutorial walks through a classical Delphi flow using synthetic data only. It is not approval for real human-subjects launch.

## Purpose

A classical Delphi study begins with open-ended Round 1 responses, curates those responses into items, and then asks participants to rate or rank the curated items in later rounds with neutral controlled feedback.

## 1. Create A Study

Open Delphi Commons and use Study Builder to create a study. Document:

- study title;
- research question;
- why Delphi is appropriate;
- planned number of rounds;
- terminal round;
- participant-facing description.

## 2. Define Panel Criteria

Document expert or stakeholder inclusion criteria. The platform should record why this panel is appropriate for the study question. Avoid vague panel definitions that cannot be reported later.

## 3. Configure Consent

Configure participant information and consent materials. Consent should explain:

- voluntariness;
- confidentiality and anonymity limits;
- withdrawal;
- what participation involves;
- expected time;
- data handling;
- contact/help path.

Consent must be explicit, versioned, and timestamped.

## 4. Configure Consensus Rule

Define the consensus rule before launch. For example:

- agreement threshold;
- rating range counted as agreement;
- dispersion rule such as IQR;
- rationale for the rule.

The rule should be locked before Round 1 and should not be adjusted after results are visible.

## 5. Launch Round 1

Round 1 should collect open-ended independent judgments. Participants should understand that there are no correct answers and that disagreement is meaningful.

## 6. Curate Round 1 Responses

After Round 1 closes, curate responses into candidate Round 2 items. Preserve provenance:

- panel-generated;
- literature-derived;
- researcher-added;
- AI-assisted draft, human approved.

Do not silently erase unique or minority perspectives. If AI assists with clustering or drafting, it must remain non-final until accepted or edited by an authorized human.

## 7. Launch Round 2 Rating

Round 2 presents curated items for rating or ranking. Participants may see neutral controlled feedback where configured. Feedback must not pressure convergence or imply that the group median is correct.

## 8. Provide Feedback

For later rounds, use compact feedback:

- participant prior response, if configured;
- anonymized group median;
- IQR or distribution;
- neutral summary, if approved;
- anonymized rationale excerpts only if approved and privacy-safe.

Participants must be free to keep or revise their response.

## 9. Final Report And Export

Generate exports and reports from the study record. The final report should include:

- method and study purpose;
- panel criteria;
- consent and governance status;
- rounds and response rates;
- attrition;
- consensus rule;
- consensus items;
- non-consensus items;
- limitations.

Required interpretation: consensus indicates agreement among this panel; it does not establish correctness.

## 10. Non-Consensus Review

Non-consensus is a valid finding. Review unresolved items and preserved minority perspectives before reporting. Do not hide non-consensus items from exports.
